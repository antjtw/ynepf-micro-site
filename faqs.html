FAQs

These answers cover how our competitions work. They do not cover the rules of lifting itself. For those, read the two official guides below before you compete.

Read these first

The rules of lifting and the list of allowed kit are set by the IPF, not by us.

  • IPF Technical Rules — how each lift is judged, and why a lift can fail
  • IPF Approved Kit List — the only kit you may use in a sanctioned competition

Before you enter

Do I need a membership to compete?

Yes. You must be a member of British Powerlifting to lift at our competitions. Buy a membership on the British Powerlifting website.

Bring proof of your membership to weigh-in. If you cannot show it, you will not be allowed to lift.

Can anyone enter, or do I need to be good enough?

Anyone can enter, from your first competition to your hundredth. The only exception is a competition that needs a qualifying total. If that applies, we say so clearly in the competition details.

What kit am I allowed to use?

You may only use kit from the IPF Approved Kit List. Anything not on that list is not allowed. Lifting straps are not allowed.

Chalk is provided on the day. Liquid chalk is not allowed.

What equipment will be on the platform?

On the competition platform we use an Eleiko rack with a soft-pad bench, and Eleiko bars. At the Implexus Open we use the host club's rack and bars.

In the warm-up area, every venue has adjustable racks, power bars and calibrated plates.

Entry, changes and refunds

How do I find out when entries open?

We post the dates and times for priority, general and guest entry on our social media channels. Watch those for the entry link.

Can I change or cancel my entry?

You can change or cancel up to the date in the competition details. This is usually the Saturday three weeks before the competition. There are no exceptions.

Can I get a refund?

Once entry closes, we cannot make changes or give refunds if you pull out. Entries cannot be moved to another event.

If you pre-ordered a media package and then pull out, we will refund that.

I am a guest lifter. What does that mean?

Guest lifters can enter one week after general entry, if spaces are left. As a guest you are not eligible to win, to place, or to set divisional records. You can still set a qualifying total for national competitions, in the weight class you lift in on the day.

Volunteering, points and discounts

Can I volunteer at a competition?

Yes, and we would love your help. Volunteers take on roles such as setting up, taking down, refereeing, platform crew, the table team and MC. You do not need experience. We give every volunteer a full briefing before you start.

When you volunteer, you earn points you can spend on future competitions. See how it works on the volunteer points page.

What is priority entry?

Priority entry opens one day before general entry for people who hold volunteer points. You get an email with the entry link automatically. Do not pass this link on to anyone else.

Check your points balance on the Volunteer Points tracker in our Linktree.

How do I use a volunteer discount?

If you have volunteer points, email YNEPFentries@gmail.com to ask for your discount code. Do this at least 24 hours before general entry opens.

Codes are for you only and cannot be added after you have paid.

On the day

When and how does weigh-in work?

Weigh-in is on competition day, two hours before your flight starts. Try to arrive as it opens, or a little earlier. Some competitions call you in lot-number order. If you miss your turn, you wait until everyone else has weighed in.

Weigh-in is single sex and private. At weigh-in we ask for your BP membership and your openers (your first weight in each lift).

What happens if I do not make my weight?

If you are over your category weight, you can try again later in the weigh-in period. You can have as many tries as you like while weigh-in is open.

If you still do not make weight, you lift as a guest. You will not be eligible to win, to place, or to set divisional records. You can still set a qualifying total for national competitions, in the weight class you lift in on the day. You start in the flight you were first given.

When do I set my rack heights and check my kit?

You set your squat and bench rack and safety heights after weigh-in, before the competition starts. Wear the shoes you will compete in when you set them.

If you want a referee to check your kit, do this at kit check, not during weigh-in. Checking kit during weigh-in slows the process down for everyone.

Are your competitions drug tested?

Yes. We are a drug-tested federation. Testing started at the Nov Open 2025, and we are working towards testing at every competition in 2026. Testing is carried out under UK Anti-Doping rules.

Can I bring a coach or handler?

Yes. You do not need a membership to coach. You can have one coach or handler in the warm-up area. Sub-Junior lifters can have two.

Coaches get a wristband at weigh-in and must wear it at all times. Anyone in the warm-up area without a wristband will be asked to return to the audience.

Can people come and watch?

Yes. Spectators are very welcome and we do not charge for entry. You can also watch our competitions live on our YouTube channel.

Records

What divisional records can I set?

We keep divisional records for Sub-Junior, Junior, Open and the Masters groups. We do not split Juniors down further.

You must make your category weight to set a record. Guest lifters and lifters who miss weight cannot set divisional records.

How much do I need to beat a record by?

You can beat a current record by as little as 0.5 kg.

Competition day checklist

Pack these the night before:

  • Singlet and long socks
  • The right underwear. Men: no boxer shorts. Women: no moulded-cup bras.
  • Squat shoes and deadlift shoes
  • Belt, knee sleeves and wrist wraps
  • A plain, round-neck T-shirt with short sleeves that cover the shoulders
  • Any warm-up kit, such as a foam roller or bands
  • Your BP membership, and a pen
  • Food and drink
  • Talc, if you use it (chalk is provided on the platform)
  • Sanitary protection, if you may need it
  • A basic first aid kit (painkillers, plasters, tape, hot and cold gels, nail clippers)
