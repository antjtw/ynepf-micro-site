Age and weight categories

This is a plain-English guide. The official IPF Technical Rules are the final word.

You compete against people of a similar age and bodyweight. This keeps competition fair.

Age categories

Your age category is based on your age during the calendar year, not your exact birthday.

Age categories and the ages they cover
CategoryAges it covers
Sub-JuniorFrom the day you turn 14, up to the end of the year you turn 18
JuniorFrom the year you turn 19, up to the end of the year you turn 23
OpenFrom the year you turn 19, with no upper limit
Master 1The year you turn 40, up to the end of the year you turn 49
Master 2The year you turn 50, up to the end of the year you turn 59
Master 3The year you turn 60, up to the end of the year you turn 69
Master 4The year you turn 70, with no upper limit

Lifting for anyone under 14 is only allowed at national competitions. Master 3 and Master 4 lifters cannot also compete in the Open class. Once you are entered, you cannot change your age category.

Weight categories: women

Your weight category is set by your bodyweight at weigh-in. Each class covers everyone up to and including its top weight.
