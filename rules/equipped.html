Equipped lifting

Most of our lifters compete classic (raw). Equipped lifting is a separate category where you can use extra supportive kit that helps you lift more.

This is a plain-English guide. All equipped kit must come from the official IPF Approved Kit List. The IPF Technical Rules are the final word.

Classic or equipped

You enter one category or the other, not both in the same competition. In classic you wear a singlet and may use a belt, knee sleeves and wrist wraps. In equipped you may also use a squat suit, a supportive shirt and knee wraps.

Everything below is allowed in equipped competitions only. All of it must come from the approved list.

The supportive kit

Squat suit

A one-piece supportive suit. The straps go over your shoulders at all times. The legs reach between 3 cm and 15 cm down your thigh. The material is a single thickness.

Supportive shirt

A supportive shirt for the bench press. It must be made of fabric, with a round neck and no zips, buttons or pockets. The sleeves end below your shoulder muscle and not past your elbow, and cannot be rolled up.

Knee wraps

Knee wraps up to 2 m long and 8 cm wide. You wear either knee wraps or knee sleeves, never both at once.

Altering your kit

You can take a suit or shirt in to fit, but only as pleats along the maker's original seams, on the inside. Any other change makes the kit illegal. A doctored suit or shirt will be rejected.

Logos on your kit

Your kit can be plain, or carry your name, your nation, or the event. At national and lower-level competitions it can also carry your club or sponsor. Other sponsor logos need IPF approval first.
