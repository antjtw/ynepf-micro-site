What counts as a failed lift

This is a plain-English guide. The official IPF Technical Rules are the final word.

Three referees judge every lift. After each one they show a white light for a good lift or a red light for a no lift. You need at least two white lights to pass.

The cards

When a lift fails, the referees raise a coloured card to show the reason. There are three colours, and each points to a type of fault.

Red card

A problem with depth or how far the bar travelled.

Red card reasons by lift
SquatBench pressDeadlift
Not deep enough, or knees not locked at the finish Bar not lowered to the chest or stomach, or it touched the belt Elbows not low enough, or not standing fully upright

Blue card

A problem with your body position or the bar moving the wrong way.

Blue card reasons by lift
SquatBench pressDeadlift
Not standing straight with knees locked at the start or finish Bouncing, downward movement, not pressing to locked arms, or arms not locked before "Start" Any downward movement before the finish, or resting the bar on the thighs

Yellow card

A problem with your feet, the signals, or contact.

Yellow card reasons by lift
SquatBench pressDeadlift
Moving your feet, a spotter helping, elbows supporting the lift, or dropping the bar Missing a signal, a spotter helping, heaving or thrusting, lifting off the bench, feet on the bench, or lowering before "Start" Missing the "Down" signal, dropping the bar, or stepping
