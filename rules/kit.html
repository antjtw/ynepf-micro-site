What you wear and use

There are rules about what you wear on the platform. This covers classic (raw) lifting, which is what most of our lifters do.

This is a plain-English guide. Some kit must come from the official IPF Approved Kit List. The IPF Technical Rules are the final word.

What you must wear

Singlet

You must wear a one-piece singlet that fits closely. The straps go over your shoulders at all times. It must come from the approved list.

You wear the same singlet for all three lifts.

T-shirt

You wear a plain, round-neck cotton t-shirt under your singlet, with short sleeves that cover your shoulders.

Underwear

Wear standard briefs made of cotton, nylon or polyester under your singlet. Women may also wear a sports bra or a commercial bra.

You cannot wear anything that gives you support, or anything made of rubber-like stretchy material.

Socks

Socks can be any colour. For the deadlift you must wear long socks that cover your shins, to protect them. Socks must not reach your knees or touch your knee sleeves. Full-length tights are not allowed.

Shoes

You must wear indoor sports shoes, lifting shoes or deadlift slippers. The sole must be flat and no more than 5 cm thick. Tie your laces or fasten your straps on the platform.

What you may use

Belt

You can wear a belt, worn over your singlet. It must come from the approved list. It can be up to 10 cm wide and 13 mm thick, with no extra padding.

Knee sleeves

You may wear neoprene knee sleeves, on your knees only. You cannot wear knee sleeves and knee wraps at the same time.

Wrist wraps

You may wear wrist wraps up to 1 m long and 8 cm wide. You cannot wear wrist wraps and sweatbands together.

Knee wraps

Knee wraps are only allowed in equipped competitions, not classic. You wear either knee wraps or knee sleeves, never both. See equipped lifting.

Medical tape

You may tape your thumbs, and your fingers and wrists within limits. You cannot use tape to give yourself extra support. If you need more tape than usual, for a medical reason, check with the referees first.

A few other rules

  • No oil, grease or lubricant on your body or kit.
  • Chalk (talc, baby powder or magnesium) is allowed on your body, but not on your wraps.
  • No hats on the platform. You may wear a hijab.
  • You may wear a necklace, but keep it out of your mouth while lifting.
