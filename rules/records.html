Records

You can set a record for a single lift or for your total. Here is how record attempts work.

This is a plain-English guide. The official IPF Technical Rules are the final word. For our divisional records, see the FAQs.

Beating a record

You can beat a current record by as little as 0.5 kg. This is the one time the weight on the bar does not have to be a multiple of 2.5 kg.

You can try for a record on any of your three attempts. If you set a record on a single lift, it also counts towards your total.

What makes a record count

For a record to be recognised, the competition must be properly sanctioned, judged by qualified referees, and use approved bars, discs and racks.

If two lifters ask for the same record weight, the one who lifts it first (by lot number) takes the record. A small amount is then added for the next lifter to beat.

Records across age groups

A strong lift can set records in more than one group at once. For example, if a Master lifter beats the Open record, it counts as both a Master record and an Open record. The same idea runs through all the age groups.

Our divisional records

We keep divisional records for Sub-Junior, Junior, Open and the Masters groups. We do not split Juniors down further. You must make your category weight to set one, guest lifters and lifters who miss weight cannot.
