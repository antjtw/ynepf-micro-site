Safety and conduct

Competitions are run to keep you safe and fair for everyone. Here is what that means on the day.

This is a plain-English guide. The official IPF Technical Rules are the final word.

Spotters keep you safe

Between two and five spotters and loaders are on the platform. They help you take the bar out and put it back. They must not touch you or the bar during the lift itself.

The one exception is safety: if a lift goes wrong and you might get hurt, the spotters will step in and take the bar, either when you ask or when the chief referee tells them to.

Injuries and the doctor

Every competition has a qualified medic on duty. If you are hurt, or look unwell in a way that could be risky, the doctor can examine you. If the doctor thinks you should not carry on, they can stop you from lifting, after talking it through with the jury. Your coach will be told.

Behaviour

Treat the platform and the sport with respect. Enter and leave calmly, and do not throw your belt down.

If a lifter or coach behaves badly near the platform, they get an official warning. If it carries on, the jury or referees can disqualify them and ask them to leave. For serious behaviour, they can be removed straight away.

Drug testing

We are a drug-tested federation, and testing is carried out under UK Anti-Doping rules. Testing started at the Nov Open 2025, and we are working towards testing at every competition in 2026.
