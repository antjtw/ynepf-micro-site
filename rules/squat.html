The squat

The squat is the first lift. You rest the bar across your shoulders, sit down until your hips are below your knees, then stand back up.

This is a plain-English guide. The official IPF Technical Rules are the final word.

How to do it

  1. Face the front. Rest the bar across your shoulders, behind your neck. It must not sit too low on your back.
  2. Grip the bar with your whole hand. Your hands and fingers stay on the bar the whole time. You do not have to wrap your thumbs.
  3. Lift the bar out of the rack and step back. The spotters can help you take it out.
  4. Stand up straight and still, with your knees locked. Wait for the chief referee.
  5. When the referee lowers their arm and says "Squat", bend your knees and sit down. Your hips must drop lower than the top of your knees.
  6. Stand back up to straight, with your knees locked. Do not bounce at the bottom or let the bar move down again.
  7. Stand still. When the referee says "Rack", put the bar back. Stay with the bar as you do.

Why a squat fails

Your squat will not count if you:

  • do not wait for the referee's "Squat" or "Rack" signal
  • do not go deep enough, your hips must drop below the top of your knees
  • bounce at the bottom, or let the bar move downward as you stand
  • do not stand up straight with locked knees at the start or end
  • step forward, backward or sideways (rocking between heel and toe is fine)
  • rest your elbows or upper arms on your legs for support
  • get help from a spotter while lifting, between the referee's signals
  • drop or dump the bar
