Weigh-in and competition day

This explains how the rules run a competition day: weighing in, the order you lift in, and how the referees judge each lift.

This is a plain-English guide. The official IPF Technical Rules are the final word. For how our competitions run day to day, see our FAQs.

Weigh-in

Weigh-in opens two hours before your group lifts, and lasts an hour and a half. You weigh in once, in a private room, with referees of your own sex. Bring photo ID.

If you are over or under your weight class, you can go back to the scales and try again, as long as you are still inside the weigh-in time.

At weigh-in you also give the weights for your first attempts. After weigh-in, you check your squat and bench rack heights and sign them off.

The round system

Everyone does their first squat, then everyone does their second, then their third. The competition then moves on to the bench press, and last of all the deadlift. It always runs in this order.

Within each round, lifters go from the lightest weight to the heaviest. If two lifters pick the same weight, the one with the earlier lot number goes first.

You have one minute to start your lift once the bar is ready and your name is called. You tell the table what weight you want next, within the time allowed.

The referees

Three referees judge each lift. They sit around the platform so they can see clearly. The chief referee sits at the front and gives the spoken signals.

After each lift, the referees show a light: white for a good lift, red for no lift. You need at least two white lights for the lift to count.

Before the competition, the referees check the bar, the discs, the scales and your kit. It is your job to get your kit checked before you lift. If you wear something that was not checked, you could lose your attempt.

Spotters and loaders

Between two and five spotters and loaders are on the platform. They load the bar, set up the bench, and keep you safe. They can help you take the bar out and put it back, but they must not help you during the lift itself.
